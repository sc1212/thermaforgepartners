Routebook Carson Systems · HVAC field service ERP

The HVAC ERP every ThermaForge client deploys. Replaces ServiceTitan, FieldEdge, Housecall Pro, Jobber. Plus three features none of them have.

Full feature list →

Core ERP

Dispatch board with route optimization, jobs and WO management, invoicing, AR aging by aging bucket, inventory and truck stock, mobile tech app (offline-capable), and real job-cost P&L by job, by tech, by truck. Plus customer history, equipment register, maintenance plans, recurring service contracts. Migration handled inside the engagement at no extra cost.

Reviews & Reputation

Auto-prompts the customer for a Google or Yelp review the moment the WO is closed. Tracks star rating per technician over time so the owner sees who's driving five-star reviews and who's accumulating drag. Surfaces 1-star alerts to the owner immediately — within minutes of the review hitting Google — so the situation gets addressed before it sits on the public listing for two weeks compounding into the next prospect's research.

Owners obsess over reputation because Google reviews are the highest-converting marketing asset an HVAC shop has. Most ERPs treat reviews as a separate $99/mo SaaS bolt-on. Routebook builds it into the dispatch loop.

Lead Source & Marketing ROI

Every inbound call gets tagged at intake with source: Google Ads, organic search, repeat customer, referral, Frontline AI inbound, NextDoor, direct mail, Facebook, Yelp paid, Yelp organic. The marketing dashboard then shows CAC by source and LTV by source side-by-side with cohort retention — what does a Google Ads customer cost to acquire, what's their average ticket on first call, what's the 24-month LTV including maintenance plan attach.

The owner stops spending on the channels that don't pay back and doubles down on the ones that do. Marketing budget gets reallocated on data, not vibes. Most HVAC shops over-spend on Google Ads by 30-50% because they have no LTV visibility — Routebook closes that gap inside the same screen they already use for dispatch.

— THE MOAT —

Frontline AI integration on the work order

When Jennifer (the Frontline AI receptionist) books a call, the work order in Routebook is created with the call recording embedded. The technician opens the WO on the truck, taps play, and listens to the customer describe the problem in their own words before they roll. They walk into the house already knowing the symptoms. First-time-fix rate climbs because the diagnosis starts before the truck arrives. Callback rate drops because the tech brought the right parts. Customer trust spikes because the tech "already knew."

This integration is structurally unavailable to ServiceTitan, FieldEdge, Housecall Pro, and Jobber. Those ERPs can integrate with third-party AI receptionist services (CallSource, AnswerForce, etc.) over a brittle API that passes a transcript at best. Routebook is the only HVAC ERP where the AI receptionist and the dispatch system are owned by the same company in the same stack — which means the integration is native, the audio file lives on the WO, and the tech experience is one tap.

This is not a feature that gets caught up to in 18 months. It's a structural advantage that requires a competitor to either build their own AI receptionist (24+ months and $5M+) or be acquired by one. By the time that happens, Routebook + Frontline shops have two years of compounded operational data and brand momentum.

Frontline AI Carson Systems · 24/7 phone coverage

Books the truck at 6:42pm Friday while your competitor's voicemail plays.

Full capability →

What Jennifer does

Answers every call — after-hours, weekends, overflow during peak dispatch. Books appointments directly into the Routebook dispatch board with truck-availability awareness. Captures lead data: name, address, system age and brand, problem description, urgency level. Transfers genuine emergencies (no heat in winter, no AC in summer with elderly occupants, gas smell, water damage) to the on-call manager. Logs every call with full transcript and audio.

The math on a $5M shop

The numbers usually pencil out to 8-12 missed calls per week between after-hours, weekend, and dispatcher-busy moments. At $400 average ticket and a 60% close rate on a booked appointment, that's roughly $2,000-$3,000 per week of missed dispatch — call it $100K-$150K per year. Frontline retails at $499/mo. The break-even is the second booked truck of the year.

What it replaces

Voicemail (which loses the call ~70% of the time on the first miss). Call-center contracts at $1.50-$3.00 per minute with no booking integration. The after-hours stress tax on the owner's spouse who has been answering the cell for fifteen years.

The production model

Short-form video for the social channels. Tech-on-the-truck content, install walkthroughs, owner-voice clips, before/after sequences, customer reaction edits. Production handled end-to-end on raw field footage you or your techs capture during the normal week.

What it produces

Roughly 12-20 short-form clips per month across the engagement, mixed across formats and platforms. Why this works for HVAC

HVAC video content has natural drama — a failed coil, a corroded compressor, a 30-year-old furnace getting replaced.
