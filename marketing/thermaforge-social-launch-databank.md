## Same-day launch order

1. Publish the highest-intent proof posts first: missed calls, dispatch leakage, first-call-recorded, 24-hour review, owner capacity.
2. Put the website link in the highest-intent placements only: LinkedIn, Facebook, Google Business Profile, and paid ads. Use comment/link-in-bio style on platforms where reach may be hurt by external links.
3. Run short-video clips daily. Keep them direct: face to camera, shop-floor B-roll, dispatch-room screen, first-call-recorded checklist.
4. Reuse every post three ways: founder text post, carousel slide, and short-video script.
5. Rotate calls to action: call for a quote, book review, send last week's missed calls, ask for a 24-hour call coverage review.

## Brand rules for every asset

- Background: deep charcoal or real shop floor image graded dark.
- Accent: copper `#D45506` only for rules, the letter A, CTAs, and small operator labels.
- Logo: TF monogram top-left or bottom-right; do not over-size it.
- Wordmark: `THERMAFORGE` with copper A, `PARTNERS` subtitle when space allows.
- Tone: direct operator language. Say dispatch, callbacks, no-heat, no-cool, call routing, revenue leakage, EBITDA, recap, succession, weekly review.
- Avoid: generic motivational lines, software-bro claims, mascot graphics, blue SaaS gradients, exaggerated income promises.

## Content recycling rules

- X: use the short hook and one hard line. Keep most under 240 characters.
- LinkedIn: use the operator story and one practical diagnostic.
- Instagram/Facebook: lead with the visual, then use a plainspoken caption.
- Reels/TikTok/Shorts: first three seconds should name the leak: “Your after-hours calls are not neutral.”
- Google Business Profile: publish local, direct service updates. Use call-focused CTAs.
- Paid: isolate one pain per ad. Do not stack missed calls, dispatch, recap, and AI in the same ad.
- SMS/email: use only opted-in lists and existing business relationships; this is a launch bank, not permission to spam.
